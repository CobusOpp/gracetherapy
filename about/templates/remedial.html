How remedial therapy can help your child


The objectives of remedial therapy are:
  • To assist each learner with learning so that they can actualize their potentials. and become an effective, integral part of society.
  • To help the learner attain social maturity.
  • To assist with communication skills.
  • To provide an intensive diagnostic study
  • To teach word-sound associations, blending and consonants.
  • To introduce simple sentence constructions and reading basic stories.
  • To teach each learner visual sequentialization and discrimination: association and blending of letters in words.
  • To teach auditory and visual discrimination skills to assist with word recognition and speech.
